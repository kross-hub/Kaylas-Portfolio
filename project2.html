TentDays

We present TentDays, a app that seamlessly allows users to plan their camping trips with minimal. TentDays allows users to search for the perfect dispersed camping location while being able to see other reviews and experiences to guide them. In addition, TentDays allows for easy planning and packing with the suggested packing list and activity suggestions. This allows users to share and collaborate on a public or private forum with friends, family, and like-minded people. Lastly, the platform supports easy and intuitive use and collaboration which ultimately will encourage outdoor enthusiasts to start utilizing the tool available to them.

Overview

The camping app platform is a fairly new market TentDays looked to find a unique approach to infiltrate the market and offer a beneficial approach to providing a camping app service. Ultimately, it was important to provide a space people could utilize to plan their camping trips that is intuitive to use.

Problem

The task at hand was to design a camping app platform that catered to the market and encouraged the targeted audience to use it. Although there are many camping apps available to users however according to research users are not utilizing their available resources. In addition to finding a new approach to making a name for ourselves in the camping app platform, our client was very concerned with camping apps providing a solution to all the users’ pain points. The viable solution resulted in our creation of TentDays, a one-stop-shop for all the users camping trip planning needs including collaboration, resources, and planning assistance.

Solution

Research

Competitive Analysis

After an in-depth analysis of its three main competitors Oh Ranger Park Finder, Recreation.gov, and Reserve America. The facts show that the three competitors have many of the same strengths with little variations which allow them to stand apart from one another. Oh, Ranger Park Finder focuses on sites in National Parks and Public Land. Recreation.gov broadens its horizons while committing to being a door to sites as well as activities. Lastly, Reserve America sets itself apart by being one of the first sites to all online reservations. It is evident that the opportunities for a new company are prevalent after a deep review of its competitors. First, it is vital that the new site will be very well organized and allow users to personalize their pages to share with the public space. Furthermore, it is important that collaboration is extended in new and creative ways such as reviews and experiences at specific sites. In addition, there is an opportunity to create a more modern site with more features and search filters. The future company should target one of these niche audiences in order to reach and help a greater depth of specified users.

Oh Ranger

Strengths

  1. Effective tool for viewing park guides for free.
  2. Relevant content and resources are available for the targeted audience.
  3. The app is free.
  4. Easy and simple to use.
  5. You dont have to make a account to utalize.

Weaknesses

  1. Organization!
  2. Only gives users the guide.
  3. There are no planning features within the app.
  4. Lots of content which can lead to confusion.
  5. Users are not able to interact with one another.

Opportunities

  1. Create a app that allows for collaboration (reviews, comments).
  2. Create personability.
  3. Create a site that is directed towards a niche market.
  4. Allow for more filters that pulls up specific sites not parks in general.
  5. Create more intuitive navigation.

Recreation.gov

Strengths

  1. Very effective tool for users who are looking for camp, rv, and cabin sites.
  2. Relevant Content and Resources are available for the targeted audience.
  3. All call to actions are easily accessible.
  4. The purpose for the app is very clear.
  5. It comes backed by a well known partners.
  6. Design is fluid.

Weaknesses

  1. Organization!
  2. The site is not very modern.
  3. Confusing navigation.
  4. There is not enough information about the sites.
  5. Not many filters are available.

Opportunities

  1. Create collaboration.
  2. Narrowing down the targeted market.
  3. Create more search filters.
  4. Create a place for reviews about sites and activities.
  5. More specific information about activities available.

User Survey

It is important that a new site breaking into the camping app market maintains the strengths found in its competitors without carrying over their weaknesses. There is room for a new competitor in the market if the said competitor is able to give enough value within their product to drive use. Targeting a new niche market for those who enjoy dispersed camping and have a need for an app to make the planning process easier for them will drive traffic to a new app on the market.

Questions

  • What did people enjoy about their camping apps?
  • What did they wish to see?
  • Would they benefit from an app that produced ease in the trip planning process?
  • To what extent were they using the current camping apps available to them?
  • What obstacles where users facing when planning their camping trips?

Findings

  • A vast majority of our users were not currently utilizing a camping app (90%).
  • A striking 46.3% did not camp, however, 80% of users said they would camp more if they found an app that alleviates their pain points during the process.
  • 100% of users said they would like to see detailed information about a site, photos, and reviews from other users in a new app.
  • All users felt that there should be more search filters.
  • Users feel confused on the navigation of their current apps causing them to either not camp or not use apps during their planning process.

Usability Testing

Changes Made After Usability Testing

Pain Point 1

When asked to view completed and recently viewed sites users were confused on where to tap in order to look at the diffrent screens. Users expressed that they couldnt tell which screen they were currently on as which led them to confusion on how to get to another screen.

Solution 1

To ratify this pain point in my second iteration I choose to add a segmented control which was easily recognized by my IOS interface users.

Pain Point 2

When asked to navigate further through the app to navigate to view a camping sites detail users were confused on the navigation to do so. Users also expressed a pain point concerning the lack of visual refrences during this process.

Solution 2

I made the navigation more intuitive by narrowing down user pathways in the form of a arrow and adding recognizable icons.In addition, visual representation was provded for each site leading to users feeling more encouraged to click on a site to view its details.

Usability Testing Round 2

Pain Point 4

When asked to navigate to view your history and completed sites users expressed confusion on how the completed sites appeared here. I was given feedback such as “how do you know they actually did a trip at this site and didn't just leave a review?” and “how do you know they didn't show up and it was raining and then they left?”.

Solution 4

In my next iteration of the history tab, I changed my verbiage located on my tabs to eliminate users’ confusion. By changing the completed tab to a reviewed tab users were no longer confused about how the sites appeared on this tab due to having reviewed this site during the prototype process. I also found this to better satisfy the portion of my MVP stating “As a returning user I would like to be able to see others reviews and my own reviews to make more informed decisions when choosing a camping site”.

Pain Point 5

When asked to navigate further through the app in order to view favorite sites as well as create a packing list for the users camping trip all users tested expressed many pain points during this step. The first being confused when they had saved a site. Again I was reminded of the value of verbiage. In order to fix this confusion, I changed the tab to “Favorites Sites” because that is what the users had done when selecting the heart to have these sites be added to this page. Furthermore, users had confusion on how to add a packing list and the original toggling between tabs in order to add a list.

Solution 5

To make this process more intuitive a “add” icon was placed on the packing list tab making navigating to create a packing list easy to locate. I found that users were successful in adding a list of 100% of the time after making this change.Furthermore, this change helped to satisfy the user story as a returning user, I want to be able to see my save information in order to keep track of future and past trips.

Pain Point 6

Finally, when asked to add information to create a packing list there was an abundance of confusion from multiple users on how to navigate through this process. Users addressed concerns on how to add a name and description for their list. Furthermore, users were unsure of the correct way to add items to their list. However, users did not express confusion on how they would share this information with others.

Solution 6

In order to satisfy the confusion during this process, new features were added after referring to IOS interface guidelines. Such as a data entry field that is more familiar and intuitive to users for both adding a name and description as well as adding items. After creating these changes 97.8% of users expressed little to no confusion during this process.

What did I learn?

It was a rewarding experience to design a product from scratch. Though the direction wasn’t clear in the beginning, each step added clarity and helped me to get closer to the final prototype. While there are still a lot of possibilities to explore when creating a dispersed camping app to benefit users this is a good start. I learned that your assumptions about users are not always true and it is important to do large amounts of well thought out research in order to confirm or prove beliefs in order to create a beneficial product for our users. A few insights during the usability tests were particularly surprising to me. Some of the elements which I thought were intuitive were found to be pain points. However, eventually, the testing and feedback allowed me to design an even better solution catered to the user’s pain points. I learned that research and testing are vital to completing any project. Furthermore, it became obvious to me that it is important to focus on the needs of the MVP before adding any other features. If I had more time, I would have considered doing more research at different steps in the process.
